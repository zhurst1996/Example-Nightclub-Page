What was important to us when creating Star Nite Club?

Location

The saying goes that the three most important factors in real estate are "location, location and location." The same concept holds true for nightclubs. Nightclubs work best in high-traffic areas where customers can come in off the street and enjoy the venue's atmosphere. The best locations also have enough parking, are easy for new customers to find, and have adequate floor space, electrical connections and restroom facilities.

Concept

The concept of the nightclub is another vital attribute that contributes toward the venture's success. Owners should develop a concept that has some staying power and caters to its target audience, even in the face of the fads and trends. For instance, a high-end wine bar typically would not do well in a working-class neighborhood, and a country-themed club would have a difficult time establishing a clientele in an urban environment.

Promotion

Since many high-traffic occasions, such as New Year's Eve or the Super Bowl, occur only once a year, nightclub owners must strive to attract customers every other day of the year. Nightclub promotions on days when traffic is traditionally low can boost a business toward profitability. Happy hour drink specials, trivia contests and karaoke machines give customers fun activities during the week while providing the business with much-needed revenue on slow days.

Staff

Successful nightclub operations extend well beyond what a single owner can handle. Professional bartenders, waitstaff, bar backs and security staff are essential to providing a smooth and entertaining time for customers. Music venues rely on sound engineers and lighting technicians to deliver a memorable visual and acoustic experience. Owners of music venues also must decide if they want to feature live bands, DJs or dancers to give the customers the sounds and atmosphere that they want.
